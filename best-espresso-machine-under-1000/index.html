Buying guide (under $1000)

If you want the best taste ceiling

Choose a semi-automatic machine and plan for a grinder path. Even a “good enough” grinder plus fresh beans beats most shortcuts.

If you want speed and zero mess

Capsules are hard to beat: consistent and fast. You trade away dialing control and accept ongoing capsule cost.

Don’t ignore water

Water hardness drives scale and taste. If your area is hard-water, filtered water reduces maintenance pain.

Milk drinks vs straight espresso

If you drink mostly lattes, prioritize steam workflow and routine convenience over “max espresso control.”

Shortcut: If you’re torn between two picks, choose the one you’ll enjoy using every day. Consistency beats “potential.”

FAQ

Do these picks stay under $1000 all year?
Prices move constantly. We select models commonly available under $1000 in Canada, but always confirm current pricing on the retailer page. If a model stays above the threshold for a long time, we update the page and replace it.
Is a built-in grinder good enough under $1000?
Often yes for starting out. The biggest improvement later is usually grind consistency, but you can make excellent drinks with a well-dialed built-in grinder and fresh beans.
What matters most for better espresso at home?
Fresh beans, consistent grinding, and repeatable puck prep. Routine matters more than specs.
Should I buy capsules or semi-auto?
Capsules: fastest, least mess, most consistent. Semi-auto: higher taste ceiling and more control if you enjoy dialing. Choose based on your daily routine.
