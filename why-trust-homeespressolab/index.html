What “trust” means here

Trust is not a vibe. It’s a process. We aim to show our work so you can make a decision that fits your routine (not just a spec sheet).

Trade-offs, not hype

Every machine is a bundle of compromises: taste, speed, milk workflow, noise, maintenance.

Workflow-first testing

We judge products by what you actually do daily: prep, dialing in, steaming, cleanup.

Canada context

Availability, pricing swings, and support realities are different here. We call that out.

How we test

Our method is built around repeatability and “home reality”: limited counter space, mornings in a hurry, and a tolerance for maintenance that varies by person.

The core checks

  • Workflow: warm-up, shot routine, milk routine, cleanup time.
  • Consistency: how stable results are across multiple sessions.
  • Ease of dialing in: forgiving vs finicky behavior.
  • Maintenance burden: descaling, backflushing, daily hygiene steps.
  • Value: what you get for the money today (not years ago).

How we keep pages current

Espresso gear changes fast: stock disappears, “good value” becomes overpriced, and support can vary by retailer. We aim to update pages so your decision isn’t based on stale conditions.

What triggers an update

  • Major availability changes in Canada.
  • Price shifts that affect value (up or down).
  • Newer models replacing old ones.
  • Meaningful changes in workflow pros/cons discovered over time.
